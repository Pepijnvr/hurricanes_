Hurricanes in Houston and new orleans

Hurricanes

America gets hit by hurricanes on average once every two years (KNMI, 2017) and this means that coastal cities must be well protected. After all, hurricanes can cause entire cities to be brought down like in 2005 when storm Katrina hit New Orleans and caused over $153 billion in damage and killed a lot of citizens. For this reason i made a website about new orleans and Houston and how vulnerable they are to hurricanes.

august 28, 2005

august 28th, 2005. Hurricane Katrina upgraded to Category 5 status with 175 mph Sustained Winds. The director of the National Hurricane Center, Max Mayfield, expressed concern that Katrina might push its storm surge over the New Orleans levees and flood walls.
1.000.000 people obey the evacuation order, 100.000 choose to stay in the city

august 29, 2005

Hurricane Katrina Makes Landfall as Category 3 status at 6:10 AM local time near Buras-Triumph, Louisiana with sustained windspeeds of 140 MPH. 53 levees were breached in greater New Orleans.
80% of the city floods and the city of new orleans has no clean water or electricity.
